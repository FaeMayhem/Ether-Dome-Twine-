The Ether Dome

October 16, 1846. Inside the Ether Dome at Massachusetts General Hospital, three men stand at the edge of medical history.

You will follow one.

Morton arrives late, reeking faintly of orange oil. The patient inhales. The surgeon cuts. No scream follows.

He sees glory — and money.

Jackson was not in the Dome that day. But he insists the idea was his.

He begins writing letters. He never stops.

Wells was first. Nitrous oxide. A failed demonstration. Laughter in the amphitheater.

Humiliation can fester.

The revolution succeeds. The men do not.

History remembers anesthesia. It does not remember peace.
